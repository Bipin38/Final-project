Yoga Asana Classification

Pose classification in yoga involves using machine learning algorithms to identify and categorize different yoga poses based on their visual features. This process typically involves training a model using a large dataset of images of individuals performing various yoga poses, and then using this model to predict the pose of new video. By accurately classifying yoga poses, this technology has the potential to improve yoga practice and help individuals better track their progress. For example, an app could use pose classification to provide real-time feedback on alignment and form, or to suggest modifications or alternative poses for those with injuries or limitations.

Feedback Generation

The feedback generation is our project aims to provide constructive feedback to users in their yoga performance. The feedback is generated based on various factors such as posture, balance and allignment. The system analyses the user's performance and compares it with the correct allignment and posture of the yoga pose.

Bhungasana

Bhujangasana, also known as the Cobra Pose, is a popular yoga posture that is often practiced as part of a yoga routine. The pose is named after the cobra, as the pose resembles the shape of a cobra with its hood raised.

Padmasana

Padmasana, also known as the Lotus Pose, is a seated yoga posture that is commonly practiced in meditation. The pose is named after the lotus flower, which is often considered a symbol of purity and enlightenment in yoga.

Tadasana

Tadasana, also known as Mountain Pose, that is often used as a starting or ending point for many other standing poses. The word "tada" in Sanskrit means mountain, which reflects the strong and stable nature of this pose.

Virbhadrasana

Virabhadrasana II (Warrior II) is a standing pose that strengthens the legs, opens the hips, and stretches the inner thighs.

Vrikshyasana

Vrikshasana, also known as Tree Pose, is a standing yoga posture that helps improve balance, stability, and concentration.

Shavasana

Shavasana, also known as Corpse Pose, is a relaxation pose commonly practiced in yoga. It is often performed at the end of a yoga practice, but can also be practiced on its own.
