Bhunjaga Asana

Bhujangasana, also known as Cobra Pose, is a popular yoga posture that is widely practiced in the Eastern world. It is named after the posture of a cobra, with the upper body lifted and the head raised. The pose is believed to activate the energy centers in the body and facilitate spiritual growth and inner awareness. Physically, Bhujangasana is an excellent pose for stretching and strengthening the muscles of the back, shoulders, and chest. It can also help to improve posture and alleviate lower back pain. To practice Bhujangasana, one should lie on their stomach with their hands placed under their shoulders and lift their chest and head while keeping their lower body grounded. Bhujangasana is deeply rooted in Eastern philosophy and tradition and can help practitioners strengthen their body, calm their mind, and tap into the spiritual energy within.

How to do it:

  1. Keep the hands and feet in place.
  2. Slide the chest forward and raise first the head, the shoulders, then, straightening the elbows, arch the back into the cobra pose. This will lower the buttocks and hips to the floor.
  3. Bend the head back and direct the gaze upward to the eyebrow centre.
  4. The thighs and hips remain on the floor and the arms support the trunk.
  5. Unless the spine is very flexible the arms will remain slightly bent.
